# Stock Analysis Framework

A strong stock usually has healthy revenue growth, positive profit margins, manageable debt, and consistent returns on equity or assets.

## Key Metrics

P/E Ratio:
Shows how much investors are paying for each dollar of earnings. A very high P/E can mean the stock is expensive unless growth is strong.

Debt-to-Equity:
Shows how much debt a company uses compared to shareholder equity. Lower is generally safer, but acceptable levels vary by industry.

Profit Margin:
Shows how much of each dollar of revenue becomes profit. Higher margins usually suggest stronger pricing power or efficiency.

Revenue Growth:
Shows whether the company is expanding. Positive growth is generally good, but it should be compared with valuation.

Recommendation Logic:
Buy means strong fundamentals and reasonable risk.
Hold means mixed signals or fair valuation.
Avoid means weak fundamentals, high risk, or poor data quality.
